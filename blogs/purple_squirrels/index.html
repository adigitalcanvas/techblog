Purple Squirrels vs. Gray Squirrels: Who Actually Builds Better Teams?

🧠 HR thinking | 🎯 Talent strategy | 🌱 Growth mindset

In HR, we often talk about the "Purple Squirrel" — that mythical candidate who ticks every single box.

It sounds ideal. But in practice, it can quietly push us into a perfection trap.

Because while we're busy searching for someone flawless, we might be overlooking people who are more than capable — people who can grow into the role, bring fresh thinking, and add real value over time.

That "gray squirrel" — the one who may not look perfect on paper — is often the one who stays, learns, adapts, and strengthens the team.

Hiring for attitude, curiosity, and potential tends to pay off far more than chasing a checklist. Skills can be developed. Mindset is harder to teach.

And let's be honest — the perfect candidate doesn't always exist. And when they do, they may not stay long enough to make the impact you expect.

Teams don't become strong because every hire was perfect. They become strong because the people in them grow, adapt, and commit.

So maybe the question isn't:
"Did we find the perfect person?"

It's:
"Did we find someone who can grow with us?"

Because more often than not, a "good enough" hire turns into a great one.

And sometimes, that Purple Squirrel?
It’s already looking for a taller tree.

If this resonates with you, the real question becomes: how do we build systems—internally and externally—that value growth over perfection?

🧩 Takeaway: Next time you're drafting a role profile, leave room for potential. The best teams are rarely built by collecting perfect resumes — they're built by people who evolve together.

People. Process. Performance. — Building teams that grow, not just perform.
